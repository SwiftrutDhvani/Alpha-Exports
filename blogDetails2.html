What is Ginger?

Ginger is a globally cherished spice, adored by chefs for its unique spicy and aromatic flavor. Whether enhancing the taste of pasta or enriching the depth of Indian curries, ginger is a culinary essential. Beyond its culinary appeal, ginger has long been recognized for its medicinal properties, aiding in alleviating nausea, improving digestion, and promoting overall well-being.

History of Ginger

Ginger boasts a rich history, tracing back to around 100 BC. Introduced to Europe by English merchants, it became a popular ingredient in jams and puddings due to its naturally sweet yet spicy flavor. Native to Southern China and Southeast Asia, ginger requires approximately 15 years to produce seeds for cultivation. Historically, ginger has been revered not only for its flavor but also for its rejuvenating and purifying properties. In folklore, it’s believed to bring good dreams when placed under a pillow.

Benefits of Ginger

Ginger is celebrated for its multitude of health benefits. Originating as a medicinal plant in Chinese culture, it has been used for centuries in both healing and spiritual practices. Let’s delve into the remarkable benefits that ginger offers.

Side Effects & Safety of
Ginger

Ginger has a few potential side effects. It’s important to consume Ginger cautiously, especially when using it for medicinal purposes. Ginger closely resembles Japanese Ginger, which is highly toxic and can cause serious health issues, including seizures.

Adulteration or mixing of Ginger with other substances has sometimes been reported, and this can be fatal, especially for infants. Consuming Ginger in unregulated amounts can lead to health complications like low blood pressure or reduced insulin levels, which can be dangerous for individuals with pre-existing conditions. Additionally, some people may experience allergic reactions to Ginger, such as blisters or swelling. Always perform a skin test before applying Ginger topically.

How to consume Ginger?

Consumption of Ginger is easy for a variety of reasons. Ginger can be consumed as a juice by boiling it in water and then cooling it down. Drinking this mixture can provide a cooling effect for the body. In Indian cuisine, Ginger is widely used as a spice, often crushed and used in its powdered form. It is also commonly used with milk in various recipes.

Ginger is also a key ingredient in the popular "masala chai". Consuming Ginger after meals can help cure indigestion and bloating naturally. It is very soothing for the stomach. Additionally, Ginger tea is known for its calming properties and is said to help improve sleep.

Export Details of Ginger

The export of Ginger has experienced both highs and lows over the years. However, with the increasing popularity of home-cooked meals during the pandemic, the export of Ginger from India has seen a slight rise. India shares Ginger production with other countries like China and Vietnam, which are the primary producers. In India, Ginger is mainly produced in the state of Arunachal Pradesh.
